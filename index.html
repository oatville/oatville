The - Ingredients

Natural Oatmeal

"Natural oatmeal is a processed product from oat seeds that has been ground into fine flakes or powder. Natural oatmeal is rich in fiber, vitamins, minerals and antioxidants. In the manufacture of oatville, natural oatmeal is used as a base to give oats its texture, nutrition and distinctive taste.".

Cocoa Fat

"Cocoa butter is a natural component extracted from cocoa beans. These fats give richness and tenderness to products like chocolate. In making oatvile, cocoa butter is used to give it a delicious chocolate flavor and a distinctive aroma."

Vegetable Creamer

"Vegetable creamer is a product used as a substitute for milk in food manufacture. Vegetable creamers are made from plant-based ingredients such as soybeans or coconut which are processed into powders or viscous liquids. In the manufacture of oatville, vegetable creamer is used to give thickness, tenderness and rich taste to the oatbar dough."

Sugar

"Sugar is a sweetener that is commonly used in food and beverages. Sugar gives the oatbars a sweet taste. In making oatville, sugar is used to enhance the pleasant taste. However, it is important to consume sugar in a balanced amount and not excessive."

Soy Lecithin

"Soy lecithin is a natural substance found in soybean seeds. Soy lecithin functions as an emulsifier, which is a substance that helps mix ingredients that normally do not mix easily, such as oil and water. In the manufacture of oatville, soy lecithin is used to maintain the consistency and texture of the dough, and to ensure that all the ingredients mix well."
